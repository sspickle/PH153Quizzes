A billiard ball on a billiards table has a momentum $\VAR{data.p}$. When a stick strikes the ball, a net force of $\VAR{data.F}$ acts on the billiard ball for $\VAR{data.t_ms}$ ($\VAR{data.t}$). What is the final momentum of the ball after it is struck? Start with a fundamental principle of physics. Show your work.


%% if ans

Solution: Use the momentum principle:

\begin{eqnarray*}
\Delta \vec{p} & = & \vec{F}_{\rm net} \cdot \Delta t \\
& = & (\VAR{data.F})(\VAR{data.t_ms}) = (\VAR{data.F})(\VAR{data.t}) = \VAR{data.dp}\\
\vec{p_f} & = & \vec{p}_i + \Delta \vec{p} = \VAR{data.p} + \VAR{data.dp} = \VAR{data.pf}
\end{eqnarray*}
